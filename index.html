I'm interested in machine learning, approximate inference, and more recently, algorithms for Riemannian geometries. If you're a student and interested in working with me on research, feel free to reach out. You can find some of my papers on arXiv or Google Scholar.

Biosketch

I'm an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Spelman College. I was a Roman Family Teaching and Research Fellow in the Computer Science Department at Barnard College. I did my PhD in the Computer Science Department at Columbia University. Most of my work has focused on developing new approaches in Bayesian inference, probabilistic models, and machine learning applications in scientific research.
